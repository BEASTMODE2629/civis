</ul><h2>
<a name="winning" class="anchor" href="#winning"><span class="mini-icon mini-icon-link"></span></a>Winning</h2>

<p>A victory can be achieved in four different ways.</p>

<ul>
<li>Domination: capture all cities of the game.</li>
<li>Culture: Obtain 20,000 culture point.</li>
<li>Economic: Acquire 20,000 Gold.</li>
<li>Technological: Be the first to discover Tecnologia Futura. </li>
</ul><p>As a civilization nears one of the above mentioned victory conditions, all other civilizations will usually declare war on it in an attempt to delay or stop it from winning.</p>

<a name="units" class="anchor" href="#units"><span class="mini-icon mini-icon-link"></span></a>Units</h2>

<p>Each unit have 5 stats:</p>

<ul>
<li>
<strong>Life:</strong> Remaining life points of the unit. If the unit have less than 1 life point, it dies.</li>
<li>
<strong>Exp:</strong> Experience points of the unit. A unit can gain Exp by surviving in a fight. When a unit reach 5 Exp, it becomes Veteran, and gain +50% Atk/Def. When a unit reach 10 Exp, it becomes Elite, and gain +100% Atk/Def.</li>
<li>
<strong>Atk:</strong> Attack power of the unit, used to deal damage in a fight.</li>
<li>
<strong>Def:</strong> Defence power of the unit, used to prevent damage in a fight.</li>
<li>
<strong>Mov:</strong> Movement points of the unit, used to move around the map.</li>
</ul><p>A unit can do various actions, depending to the unit type. Actions can be:</p>

<ul>
<li>
<strong>Move:</strong> Move the unit to another tile. To move, a unit requires Mov points and consumes a Mov point every tiles moved. Some tiles consumes more Mov than others, see the section <em>The Map</em> for futher informations.</li>
<li>
<strong>Fortify:</strong> Fortify the unit, that gain a +25% Atk/Def until it's fortified. Moving the unit de-fortify the unit.</li>
<li>
<strong>Kill:</strong> Kill the unit to gain a small amount of gold. The unit will be removed.</li>
<li>
<strong>No Orders:</strong> Issue no orders to the unit, that will be left as it is.</li>
<li>
<strong>Settle:</strong> Settle a city in the tile where the unit is. Only available for settlers.</li>
</ul><h3>
<a name="fighting" class="anchor" href="#fighting"><span class="mini-icon mini-icon-link"></span></a>Fighting</h3>

<p>A fight is resolved this way:</p>

<ol>
<li>Each unit deal damage to the other unit, and the damage dealt is egual to the Atk of the unit minus the Def of the other unit (Damage dealt = Atk - Def).</li>
<li>The damage dealt decrease the life points of the units, and if a unit have no remaining life points, it dies. In a fight could die one, both or none of the units.</li>
<li>The surviving units (if any) gain an Exp. If a unit is promoted this way to the Elite or Veteran rank, the max life of the unit will be doubled and all the life points will be restored.</li>
<li>If the attacker kill the defender, it take its position. By the way, if the attacker get killed by the defender, the defender cannot gain the position, and stand on its tile.</li>
</ol><h3>

<h2>
<a name="science" class="anchor" href="#science"><span class="mini-icon mini-icon-link"></span></a>Science</h2>

<p>Science is produced by the player's cities and is used to pay the cost of the research of technologies.<br>
Each turn a city produce science and the total science from all of the player's cities is used to pay the cost of the player current technology research.<br>
A city produce science equal to the half of its commerce production, plus eventual bonus granted by buildings, tiles, technologies and societies.<br>
If no research is in queue at the end of the turn, the science produced is converted to gold.</p>
